The bathroom was the second room I tackled, after my wife's office/safe haven room.

Unfortunately, I don't have any pictures of what it looked like before, but basically it was an empty room which used to be a childrens nursery. 

It is at the back of the house with a window looking out across to a quiet hill on the other side of the beck, and the old owners for some reason had the bright idea to put the bathroom in the middle of the house, with no window or ventilation, so it was getting mouldy. Genius!

So I took it upon myself to make a new bathroom in this room which used to be a nursery, and then rip the existing bathroom completely out, and turn that into a kids nursery. Was I insane? No. Am I now after doing it? Definitely. Was it worth it? I think so.

Here is some footage of what the bathroom area looked like just after I'd started the work:

![Bathroom Before](/images/bathroom-before.gif "Bathroom Before")

I chose to make the room slightly smaller than it was before, in order to increase the size of the soon to be new kids nursery in the middle of the house. To do this, I had to build a stud wall, which I chosen to put a window in too so light could come through a frosted glass plane into my future childs nursery, because I didn't want a mole kid. This stud wall had to then be tanked to stop moisture penetrating the plasterboard and studwork. 

After the stud wall was done, the rest of the job was plastering, tiling and tee-ing of the rising main and hot water supply pipes that were passing through the joist space under the floorboards to bring hot and cold water into the room.

I then installed a sink, toilet and bath and plumbed those in. The toilet waste I installed myself, but had to get a contractor in to run the waste pipe on the external of the house to the municupal waste pipe as that was quite high up and to be honest I was fucking knackered at that point lol.
