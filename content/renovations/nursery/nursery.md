The nursery was the third room I tackled, after the [New Bathroom][1]

So, this *used* to be the old bathroom, but as I mentioned in the new bathroom post, that was a profoundly stupid idea because it's in the middle of the house, with no windows, so didn't get much air flow, and therefore, was prone to getting moldy, because you know, bathroom get moist...

So, first job in here was smashing all the tiles off the walls, lifting out the toilet, bath and shower. I found out here that the previous owners had plumbed the toilet waste in the shower waste, literally the same pipe... and, the pipe had no drop on it, so shit (literally) was sitting in that pipe going fetid, and that pipe was connected to the shower waste, so when you took a shower, the room filled up with shit stink... Probably highly illegal and yada yada, whatever, it's all moot now coz I ripped the fucker out and threw it in a skip lol.

Here's what that room looked like then, after I'd done the smashy smash:

![Nursery 1][2]

After that, it was "just" a case of plasterboarding the other side of the stud wall I'd put up when I did the new bathroom, putting bonding plaster on the exposed brickwork, then plastering the whole room, then putting skirting and coving up and decorating, and finally installing shelving (because I wanted more storage space)

I'm quite a handy guy it turns out, when I put my mind to it! Also, that frosted glass panel I put in the stood wall is really helping get some light into this otherwise dingy room, so that was quite smart too, if I may say so :)
